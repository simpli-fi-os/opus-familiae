# CLAUDE.md - Opus Familiae (The Great Work of Family)

## Project Overview

Opus Familiae empowers young couples to build sufficient financial independence in 5 to 10 years to step into an intentional season of devoted family life during the first 15 years of their children's development. Three pillars: Save, Invest, Earn. One mission: time with your children when it matters most.

**Founders:** Hunter & Lindsey Lott (Denton, TX)
**Stage:** Pre-launch. White paper complete. No product, no content, no audience yet. Founders living the mission.
**Priority:** #2 in the Simpli-FI ecosystem (behind TSI). Content creation is the immediate action.
**Score:** 76/100 on 10-dimension venture scorecard.

## The Venture

### What Opus Familiae IS
- A content, community, and referral platform for young families pursuing financial independence for family seasons
- A cultural reclamation of the "OF" monogram (dignity over degradation)
- The Lott family's real-time, public proof of concept
- Part of the Simpli-FI ecosystem (one of ten interconnected ventures)

### What Opus Familiae IS NOT
- A lifestyle brand selling the fantasy of passive income
- An early retirement movement
- A generic parenting blog or financial education company
- A standalone business (it is deeply integrated with the ecosystem)

### The Three Pillars
1. **Save:** Aggressive financial discipline (30-50% savings rate, debt elimination, career optimization). Ages 18-30 sprint phase.
2. **Invest:** Deploy capital through qualified advisors (Simpli-FI Alpha LLC) into income-generating assets. Build passive income to replace one salary.
3. **Earn:** Monetize family life through the OF creator ecosystem. Courses, community, content. The OF Alternative to exploitative platforms.

### The Family Office Team of Experts
Seven functional areas delivered via content, community, and curated referrals:
1. Financial Planner (OF content + community)
2. Investment Advisor (Simpli-FI Alpha, direct referral, SEC-compliant solicitor agreement)
3. Tax Strategist (Simpli-FI Admin AI-powered tax intelligence)
4. Estate Planner (TSI vetted provider directory)
5. Insurance Specialist (TSI vetted provider directory)
6. Career & Creator Coach (OF community + Simpli-FI OS web services)
7. Family Life Educator (Learning Odyssey + OF content)

## The Lott Family Story

This is the emotional and strategic core of all OF narrative content. Use it intentionally, not scattered.

Hunter and Lindsey were both only children from broken homes. Neither came from means. They paid for their own college and built everything from scratch. Hunter's dad recently got out of prison. His mom had to sell her home after a divorce. Lindsey is a former Pediatric ER Nurse (still certified) who chose full-time stay-at-home motherhood and homeschool coordination. Four children: Remington (leads web services under Simpli-FI OS), Juday, Avonlea, Callahan. Lindsey's mom lives with them.

They gave away their second vehicle to a young single mom trying to complete her GED. They carry ~$502K in total debt (mortgage + one car). Goals: debt-free by Dec 2026, 5-year travel homeschool season (motorhome, plane, sailboat), forever home and family land, care for aging parents.

Almost every venture traces back to a pain point they lived. TSI came from trying to help a single mom at the fire station. Alpha came from wanting families to access institutional investment strategies. Life came from Lindsey needing systems for four kids.

## Content Strategy

### Content Pillars
1. **The Journey** - Real-time Lott family progress: debt payoff, building, setbacks, wins
2. **The Framework** - Save/Invest/Earn pillar education, tactical how-tos
3. **The Community** - Member spotlights, accountability stories, milestone celebrations
4. **The Alternative** - Cultural commentary on what "OF" should mean, family vs. transaction economy
5. **The Integration** - How OF connects to TSI, Alpha, Life, Learning Odyssey (ecosystem awareness)

### Content Calendar Rhythm
- YouTube: 1-2 videos/week (phone quality is fine, authenticity over production)
- Podcast: 1 episode/week (audio version of YouTube content + deeper conversations)
- Email: 1 newsletter/week to subscriber list
- Social: 3-5 posts/week (Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok clips from longer content)

### Tone and Voice
- Casual but authoritative. Smart friend over coffee, not corporate consultant.
- Vulnerable without being performative. Real struggle, not manufactured drama.
- Faith-informed but not preachy. Scripture lands in moments of reflection, not as decoration.
- Never use em dashes. Never use AI-sounding phrases. (See writing rules below.)

## Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **NEVER use em dashes (--).** Use commas, periods, colons, or restructure. This is the #1 rule.
2. **NEVER use:** "It's important to note," "Let's dive in," "In today's fast-paced world," "At the end of the day," "leverage" (as verb), "unlock" (as metaphor), "navigate" (unless literal), "harness," "game-changer," "cutting-edge," "seamlessly," "robust" (unless physical), "synergy," "holistic" (unless medical). Nothing that sounds like a LinkedIn post.
3. **Tone:** Casual, authoritative, direct, confident. Not corporate. Not preachy. Not salesy.
4. **Structure:** Prose and paragraphs over bullet lists. When bullets are needed, make them 1-2 sentences each.
5. **Historical references welcome:** Roosevelt, Disney, Churchill, Lincoln, Patton, Chesty Puller, Marcus Aurelius, Shackleton, Paul. Use when earned, not as decoration.
6. **Scripture:** ESV translation. 1 Peter 4:10 is the anchor verse. Use sparingly and meaningfully.
