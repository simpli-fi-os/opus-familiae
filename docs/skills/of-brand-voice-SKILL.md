# Opus Familiae Brand Voice

> *"A brand is a living entity, and it is enriched or undermined cumulatively over time, the product of a thousand small gestures."*
> — Michael Eisner

## Skill Overview

This skill guards the Opus Familiae brand voice across every touchpoint: content, copy, communications, marketing, partnerships, and internal documents. It ensures that OF always sounds like OF, regardless of which Claude instance, which collaborator, or which platform is producing the content. Consistency is not just an aesthetic preference. It is how trust compounds.

---

## The OF Voice: Who Are We Talking?

Opus Familiae sounds like Hunter and Lindsey Lott sitting across a kitchen table from a young couple, coffee in hand, kids in the background, being honest about money, family, and what it actually takes.

### Voice Attributes

**Casual but authoritative.** We talk like friends, not consultants. But we know what we're talking about because we've lived it. The confidence comes from experience, not credentials (though we have those too).

**Vulnerable without being performative.** We share real numbers, real setbacks, real fears. We never manufacture drama for content. When something is hard, we say it's hard. When something worked, we say it worked. The audience can tell the difference between honesty and performance.

**Direct without being harsh.** We don't soften every hard truth with three caveats. If a strategy is wrong, we say so. If a sacrifice is necessary, we name it. But we always deliver the hard truth with respect and care. We're the friend who tells you what you need to hear, not what you want to hear.

**Faith-informed, never preachy.** Our faith is central to why we do this. 1 Peter 4:10 is the anchor verse. Scripture appears in moments of reflection, clarity, and encouragement. Never as a sales tool. Never as judgment. Never forced into technical or tactical content. The way a wise friend would mention it in conversation, not the way a bumper sticker would.

**Mission-driven, never salesy.** We are building something we believe in. The conviction comes through in every sentence. But we never pressure, never create false urgency, never use scarcity tactics. The mission sells itself to the right families. We just need to be honest about what it is.

### Voice Anti-Patterns (What OF Never Sounds Like)

- **Corporate:** "We are proud to announce our community membership offering designed to optimize your family's financial trajectory." (No.)
- **Bro-finance:** "Ready to crush your debt and stack paper? Let's gooooo." (No.)
- **Mommy-blogger:** "Hey mama! I know life is SO crazy right now but we are just SO blessed." (No.)
- **LinkedIn thought leader:** "I've been thinking a lot about this. Here are 7 lessons on leadership and family. Thread." (No.)
- **Prosperity gospel:** "God wants you to be wealthy. Claim your abundance." (Absolutely not.)
- **Hustle culture:** "Sleep is for the weak. Grind now, rest later." (The opposite of our message.)

## The OF Alternative: Cultural Reclamation Framework

### The Core Positioning

The monogram "OF" carries a dominant cultural association: OnlyFans, a platform built on the commodification of intimacy and parasocial relationships. Opus Familiae deliberately reclaims those two letters.

**The positioning is:**
- Same monogram
- Entirely different meaning
- Not parody, not mockery, not outrage
- A statement of conviction that the most compelling content a person can create is the story of building something real

### How to Talk About the OF Alternative

**Do:**
- State the contrast clearly and let the audience draw their own conclusions
- Frame it as a positive alternative, not as an attack on the other platform
- Emphasize what OF IS rather than what it's against
- Use the contrast to spark conversation and discovery
- Let the cultural tension do the marketing work

**Don't:**
- Mock or shame people who use the other platform
- Use explicit sexual references or imagery, even as contrast
- Make it the entire brand identity (it's a positioning tool, not the mission)
- Overexplain. The audience gets it. Trust them.

### Messaging Examples

**Strong:** "Two letters. Two very different propositions. One sells access to strangers. The other invests in the people who matter most."

**Strong:** "The OF economy is worth $6 billion. What if those same two letters stood for something that lasts?"

**Strong:** "Opus Familiae: The Great Work of Family. Because the most compelling thing you can offer the world isn't manufactured closeness with strangers. It's the real thing with the people you love."

**Weak:** "Unlike OnlyFans, we believe in family values." (Too preachy, too judgmental, too simplistic.)

**Weak:** "OF should stand for something better!" (Vague, sounds like a petition, not a brand.)

## Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable, Apply to All OF Content)

### Rule 1: No Em Dashes

This is the single most important rule. Em dashes (— or --) are the number one giveaway of AI-generated text. Replace with commas, periods, colons, semicolons, or restructure the sentence.

**Before (wrong):** "We carry $502K in debt — mortgage, car, and credit cards — but we're on track."
**After (right):** "We carry $502K in debt. Mortgage, car, and credit cards. But we're on track."

**Before (wrong):** "The mission is simple — build financial independence so you can be present."
**After (right):** "The mission is simple: build financial independence so you can be present."

### Rule 2: Banned Phrases

Never use any of the following in OF content:

| Banned Phrase | Why | Alternative |
|--------------|-----|-------------|
| "It's important to note" | Filler, adds nothing | Just state the thing |
| "Let's dive in" | Generic, overused | Start with the content |
| "In today's fast-paced world" | Cliche | Be specific about what's changed |
| "At the end of the day" | Filler | Cut it |
| "Leverage" (as verb) | Corporate | "Use," "apply," "build on" |
| "Unlock" (as metaphor) | AI-speak | "Access," "earn," "open" |
| "Navigate" (unless literal) | Corporate therapy | "Handle," "work through," "manage" |
| "Harness the power of" | Marketing fluff | Just name the thing |
| "Game-changer" | Overused to meaninglessness | Be specific about the impact |
| "Cutting-edge" | Buzzword | Describe what makes it new |
| "Seamlessly" | AI-speak | "Smoothly," "naturally," or cut |
| "Robust" (unless physical) | Tech-speak | "Strong," "thorough," "solid" |
| "Synergy" | Corporate | "Connection," "reinforcement" |
| "Holistic" (unless medical) | Wellness-speak | "Complete," "comprehensive," "whole" |
| "Empower" (used alone) | Empty without specifics | Name the specific capability being granted |

### Rule 3: Prose Over Bullets

Default to paragraphs. Use bullet lists only when the content genuinely requires enumeration (actual lists, actual steps, actual options). When bullets are used, each bullet should be 1-2 full sentences, not fragments.

### Rule 4: Historical References and Scripture

**Historical figures who resonate:** Theodore Roosevelt, Walt Disney, Winston Churchill, Abraham Lincoln, George Patton, Chesty Puller, Marcus Aurelius, Nelson Mandela, Ernest Shackleton, the Apostle Paul.

Use when earned. A Roosevelt quote after Hunter ships a product means something. A Roosevelt quote before he's done anything is motivational wallpaper.

**Scripture:** ESV translation only. 1 Peter 4:10 is the anchor verse. Proverbs 13:22 for generational wealth. Colossians 3:23 for work ethic. Use sparingly. Let it land in moments of reflection, decision-making, or genuine encouragement.

## Audience-Specific Messaging Adaptation

### For Young Couples (Primary Audience)

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. "We're doing this too. Here's what we're learning."
**Emphasis:** The journey, the real numbers, the practical steps
**Avoid:** Lecturing, parenting from a pedestal, assuming their situation

### For Churches and Faith Communities

**Tone:** Service-oriented. "We built this to serve families in your congregation."
**Emphasis:** Stewardship, community, TSI integration, faith foundation
**Avoid:** Prosperity gospel language, denominational specificity, pressure

### For Financial Professionals (Team of Experts)

**Tone:** Professional peer. "Here's the client pipeline and the mission behind it."
**Emphasis:** Community size, referral quality, compliance infrastructure, TSI vetting
**Avoid:** Overexplaining the mission, emotional appeals without data

### For Potential Brand Partners

**Tone:** Business case with mission alignment. "Here's who we reach and why they trust us."
**Emphasis:** Demographics, engagement metrics, audience quality, mission alignment filter
**Avoid:** Desperation, over-promising reach, compromising on alignment

### For Investors or Strategic Partners

**Tone:** Builder-to-builder. "Here's what we've built, where it's going, and how it connects."
**Emphasis:** Ecosystem integration, revenue architecture, TAM, the flywheel
**Avoid:** Hype, unsubstantiated projections, downplaying risks

## Brand Identity (Visual: TBD)

The OF visual brand has not been designed yet. When it is, follow the pattern established by the TSI Brand Lookbook (TSI-Brand-Lookbook.html in project files). Key decisions to make:

1. **Color palette:** Should evoke warmth, trust, aspiration. Not corporate blue. Not pastel mommy-blog. Consider warm earth tones, deep greens, or rich navy with gold accents.
2. **Typography:** Serif for headings (trust, timelessness). Clean sans-serif for body. The OF monogram needs a distinctive treatment.
3. **The OF Monogram:** This is the single most important visual asset. It must be elegant, bold, and unmistakably intentional. It needs to hold its own against the cultural association it's reclaiming.
4. **Photography style:** Real, warm, slightly imperfect. Never stock photography. Always the actual Lott family or actual community members. Natural light preferred.
5. **Iconography:** Simple, warm, hand-drawn quality over corporate clean.

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any OF content, run through this:

- [ ] Does it sound like Hunter and Lindsey, not a marketing department?
- [ ] Could a 25-year-old couple with $80K in student debt read this and feel welcome, not lectured?
- [ ] Zero em dashes?
- [ ] Zero banned phrases?
- [ ] If scripture is used, is it earned and natural, not forced?
- [ ] If the OF Alternative positioning is used, is it confident without being combative?
- [ ] Does the tone match the audience (young couple vs. church vs. professional)?
- [ ] Is every sentence doing work? Cut anything that's just filling space.
- [ ] Would Hunter read this out loud without cringing?
- [ ] Does it serve the mission or just sound good?
