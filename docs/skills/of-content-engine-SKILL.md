This skill produces content for the Opus Familiae venture: YouTube scripts, podcast episodes, blog posts, email sequences, social media, course material, and lead magnets. Every piece of OF content serves three purposes: attract families to the OF community, demonstrate the Save/Invest/Earn pillars in real time, and tell the Lott family story as the proof of concept.

## The OF Content Universe

### Five Content Pillars

Every piece of OF content maps to one of these pillars. Most content touches two or three.

**1. The Journey** (40% of content)
The Lott family's real-time progress. Debt payoff milestones, setbacks, wins, behind-the-scenes ecosystem building, family moments, hard decisions. This is the emotional core. Vulnerability without performance. Real struggle, not manufactured drama.

**2. The Framework** (25% of content)
Tactical education on Save/Invest/Earn. Budget templates, debt elimination strategies, investment fundamentals, creator economy how-tos, career negotiation tactics. Actionable, specific, grounded in the Lotts' actual experience.

**3. The Community** (15% of content)
Member spotlights, accountability stories, milestone celebrations, pod updates. (Starts in Phase 2 when community launches.) Early on, this pillar features guest families or couples on a similar path.

**4. The Alternative** (10% of content)
Cultural commentary on what "OF" should mean. The dignity economy vs. the attention economy. Family formation as creative work. Why the most compelling content is building something real. This is the provocative, shareable content that drives discovery.

**5. The Integration** (10% of content)
How OF connects to the broader ecosystem. TSI, Alpha, Life, Learning Odyssey. Not sales pitches. Stories about how a firefighter family accesses the same services as a millionaire's family office. The "Family Office for the Masses" narrative.

## Content Formats

### YouTube Videos

**Structure for a standard OF YouTube video (8-15 minutes):**

1. **Cold Open (0:00-0:30):** One compelling sentence or moment. No "Hey guys welcome back." Start mid-story or with a provocative statement. Pull the viewer in before they can scroll.

2. **The Hook (0:30-1:30):** State the problem or question clearly. "We owe $502,000. Here's exactly what we paid off this month and what it cost us." Or: "Every financial advisor says 'start early.' None of them tell you HOW when you're making $59K with four kids."

3. **The Content (1:30-10:00):** Deliver value. Use the Lott family's real numbers, real decisions, real systems. Show, don't just tell. Walk through the spreadsheet. Show the bank account. Explain the decision. This section should teach something concrete that the viewer can apply to their own life within 24 hours.

4. **The Connection (10:00-12:00):** Connect this specific topic to the bigger OF mission. Where does this fit in Save/Invest/Earn? How does this serve the family season goal? Keep it brief and genuine.

5. **The Call (12:00-end):** One clear next step. Subscribe, join the email list, check out the community. Never more than one CTA.

**Production notes:**
- Phone quality is acceptable and even preferred. Authenticity over production value.
- Natural light, real locations (kitchen table, fire station, car between errands).
- Hunter and Lindsey together on camera is the highest-performing format.
- B-roll of family life (kids, organizing projects, meal prep, homeschool) over voiceover adds texture.
- No intro music longer than 3 seconds. No branded intros until 10K+ subscribers.

**Video title formulas that work for OF:**
- "We Paid Off $X This Month. Here's How." (Journey)
- "The $59K Strategy: How a Firefighter Builds Wealth" (Framework)
- "Why We Gave Away Our Car (And What Happened Next)" (Journey + Alternative)
- "The Other OF: Building Something That Lasts" (Alternative)
- "7 Things We Cut From Our Budget (And 3 We Refused To)" (Framework)

### Podcast Episodes

**Structure for a standard OF podcast (20-40 minutes):**

1. **Opening (0:00-2:00):** Brief personal update. What happened this week in the Lott household. One sentence on what the episode covers.

2. **The Meat (2:00-30:00):** Deep exploration of the topic. More conversational than YouTube. Hunter and Lindsey talking through a decision, a framework, a guest conversation, or a lesson learned. Podcast allows for longer, more nuanced discussion.

3. **The Takeaway (30:00-35:00):** One or two actionable items the listener can implement this week.

4. **Close (35:00-end):** Thank the listener. One CTA. Brief preview of next episode.

**Podcast formats that work for OF:**
- Hunter + Lindsey conversation (most episodes)
- Solo Hunter: deep dives on Invest/Earn pillars, ecosystem updates
- Solo Lindsey: organizing systems, homeschool, family management, the Life connection
- Guest interviews: other families on the financial independence path, Team of Experts providers

### Email Sequences

**Welcome Sequence (5 emails over 10 days after lead magnet download):**

Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the lead magnet. Introduce Hunter and Lindsey in one paragraph. State the OF mission in one sentence.

Email 2 (Day 2): The Lott family story. Short version: broken homes, no means, built from scratch, four kids, $502K debt, building the blueprint. End with: "We're not the family that figured it out and then started teaching. We're the family building it in public, right now."

Email 3 (Day 5): The three pillars explained. Save, Invest, Earn. One paragraph each. Link to the best YouTube video for each pillar.

Email 4 (Day 7): The OF Alternative. Cultural reclamation message. Why these two letters matter. Why building something real beats selling something ephemeral.

Email 5 (Day 10): Community invitation. What the OF community offers. Founding member pricing if available. If community isn't launched yet, invite them to reply with their family's biggest financial challenge.

**Weekly Newsletter Structure:**
- One personal update (2-3 sentences)
- One tactical insight from the week (the main content, 200-400 words)
- One link to the latest YouTube/podcast
- One question for the reader (builds reply engagement)

### Social Media

**Twitter/X:** 3-5 posts per week. Mix of:

**TikTok:** 3-5 short clips per week, repurposed from YouTube and podcast.

## The Lott Family Story: Usage Rules

The family story is the emotional core of OF content. Use it intentionally.

**When to use the full story:** Brand introductions, welcome sequences, keynote-style content, the "About" page, investor or partner materials.

**When to use fragments:** Regular content should reference specific moments, not retell the whole narrative. "When we gave away our car..." or "Hunter's dad just got out of prison, and that changed how we think about generational wealth..." These fragments are more powerful than the full story repeated.

**What to never do:**
- Never dramatize or embellish. The real story is powerful enough.
- Never use the family's hardship as a sales tactic. The story earns trust; it does not close deals.
- Never discuss the children's personal struggles or challenges publicly. They are part of the story as a family, not as individual case studies.
- Never fabricate emotional peaks. If a moment was hard, say it was hard. If it wasn't, don't pretend it was.

**Reference:** Before writing any narrative OF content, consult the Lott Family Story Reference file in project outputs.

## Writing Rules (Enforced on All OF Content)

These are non-negotiable. Every piece of OF content follows these rules.

1. **NEVER use em dashes (--).** Use commas, periods, colons, or restructure. This is the single most important rule. Em dashes are the #1 giveaway of AI-generated text.

2. **NEVER use these phrases:** "It's important to note," "Let's dive in," "In today's fast-paced world," "At the end of the day," "leverage" (verb), "unlock" (metaphor), "navigate" (unless literal), "harness," "game-changer," "cutting-edge," "seamlessly," "robust" (unless physical), "synergy," "holistic" (unless medical), anything that sounds like a LinkedIn post.

3. **Tone:** Casual but authoritative. Smart friend over coffee. Direct, confident, never corporate, never preachy, never salesy. Vulnerable without being performative.

4. **Structure:** Prose over bullets. When bullets are necessary, make them 1-2 sentences each, not fragments.

5. **Historical figures welcome:** Roosevelt, Disney, Churchill, Lincoln, Patton, Chesty Puller, Marcus Aurelius, Shackleton, Paul. Use when earned.

6. **Scripture:** ESV translation. 1 Peter 4:10 is the anchor verse. Use sparingly, meaningfully.

## Quality Checklist (Before Publishing Any OF Content)

- [ ] Does this serve the OF mission (financial independence for family seasons)?
- [ ] Does it map to at least one of the five content pillars?
- [ ] Is it actionable? Can the audience do something with this within 24 hours?
- [ ] Does it sound like Hunter and Lindsey, not like a marketing department?
- [ ] Zero em dashes?
- [ ] Zero AI-sounding phrases?
- [ ] If it references investments or Alpha, are compliance disclaimers included?
- [ ] If it tells the family story, does it do so with honesty and restraint?
- [ ] Would Hunter be proud to put his name on this five years from now?
